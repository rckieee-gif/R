# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two primary user groups share the same interface:

- **Farm owner/manager (AdminOwner, OperationManager):** Checks daily operations, batch performance, financials, harvest data, and employee pay from desktop or mobile. Needs fast access to summaries and the ability to drill into detail. Often reviewing data end-of-day or during planning.
- **Farm staff (DataEntry):** Logs daily records on-site: mortality counts, feed consumption, egg/meat harvests, inventory movements. Works from a phone or tablet in the field. Needs clear forms that are hard to misuse and fast to complete.

A public viewer mode gives read-only access to the current batch for external stakeholders.

## Product Purpose

Octavio Poultry Farm Manager is an operations management tool for a poultry farm. It tracks batch lifecycles from start to harvest, daily logs (mortality, feed, weight), employee management and pay, financial transactions, inventory, and analytics. Success looks like: the owner has full visibility into farm performance at a glance, staff can log data in under a minute, and financial records are accurate enough to print.

## Brand Personality

Reliable, Professional, Efficient.

The interface should feel like a well-run operation: structured, trustworthy, and fast. No flash for the sake of flash. Every element earns its place by making the user's job faster or clearer.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS templates with purple-to-blue gradients and identical card grids
- Overly playful or toy-like interfaces that undermine the seriousness of farm operations
- Cluttered, information-overloaded dashboards where nothing has priority
- Slow, heavy interfaces that feel sluggish on mobile (staff logs data in the field)

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration.** Every screen answers "what do I need to know right now?" before anything else. If a visual element doesn't help the user act, it goes.
2. **Field-ready.** The daily workflow (log entry, harvest recording, inventory) must work on a phone in bright sunlight with dirty hands. Large tap targets, high contrast, minimal steps.
3. **Trust through precision.** Financial data, batch records, and harvest numbers must look precise and authoritative. The interface should feel like it takes the numbers as seriously as the owner does.
4. **Progressive disclosure.** Show summaries first, details on demand. The owner sees the overview; drill-down is one tap away. Staff sees only what they need for their current task.
5. **Consistent vocabulary.** Batch, log, harvest, ledger: terms match across every screen, label, and action. No synonyms, no ambiguity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA as a practical target (4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text)
- Dark and light mode already supported; both must maintain contrast standards
- Touch targets sized for field use (minimum 44px)
- Reduced motion preference respected where animations exist
